\section*{Purpose and Reproducibility}

This supporting information records the numerical details behind the relative memory-adequacy results reported in the main text. The main manuscript keeps the scientific design compact: a resolved trajectory archive, competing transition mechanisms, and held-out observables. The tables and figures below provide the audit trail for that design, including proxy-observable sensitivity, geometric-support diagnostics, archive-density and segment-length diagnostics, the tight OpenFOAM resolution ladder, particle-tracking tolerances, objective-weight sensitivity, and the evidence matrix used to construct the relative retained-state map.

The OpenFOAM cases use voxel finite-volume meshes extracted from the same Core2 Bentheimer subvolume. The 18~$\mu$m and 12~$\mu$m cases use downsampled voxel meshes, while the 6~$\mu$m case uses the full image-resolution voxel mesh and a strict SIMPLE solve with zero linear-solver relative tolerances and residual-control thresholds of $10^{-9}$ for pressure and velocity. All manuscript-facing trajectory archives contain 20,000 particles. The OpenFOAM archives use 4000 saved steps and saved-step interval $\Delta t=0.1$. Particle integration used internal substepping with physical tolerances scaled across the resolution ladder; no case reached the substep cap.

Mechanisms with the lowest mean objective in these tables should not be read as statistically separated closures. Several mean differences are small compared with split-to-split variability, and separate columns report mean rank and split wins where available. The tables support qualitative claims: breakthrough and the occupied-volume proxy can disagree, lowest-mean mechanisms and mixture weights shift with objective and regime, OpenFOAM preserves velocity autocorrelation under refinement, and no retained state wins universally.

The occupied-volume and encounter quantities are proxy observables. The dilution index used here is an entropy-based occupied-volume measure computed from binned particles, not a resolved concentration field or scalar-dissipation calculation. Encounter probability is a coarse-grained encounter-opportunity proxy for sampled particle pairs, not a reactive-transport rate law. We therefore report reference-split, bin-size, encounter-radius, inlet-neighborhood pair-sampling, archive-density, segment-length, and geometric-support sensitivity checks in Figures~\ref{fig:Sproxy}--\ref{fig:Sarchiveconv} and Tables~\ref{tab:Sproxy}--\ref{tab:Sgeom}. These checks support qualitative interpretation of the memory map, not calibrated reactive-transport prediction.
